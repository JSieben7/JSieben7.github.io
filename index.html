    About me:

    No matter my job title, I'll always be an astronomer. During my PhD, I helped create the Star Formation Across Cosmic Time (SFACT) survey at Indiana University. This involved using the WIYN telescope to take optical and spectroscopic data of wide swaths of the sky. To find out more about my research interests, check out my research page.

    In addition to doing science, I've developed a passion for writing about science. I was formerly an Editor-at-Large for ScIU, a grad-student run and peer-edited science blog at Indiana University. You can find all the blogs I've written on my author page. Currently, I am an Associate Editor with Physics Today.

    In my free time, I enjoy baking, making youtube videos, photography, writing fantasy stories, and traveling. So many hobbies and not nearly enough time to do them all.
